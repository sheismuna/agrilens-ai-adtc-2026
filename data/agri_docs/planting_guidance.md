# Maize Planting Guidance

## Timing
Plant at the onset of reliable rains, once at least 25 to 30 mm of rain has
fallen within a short period and the soil is moist at planting depth.
Planting too early into dry soil risks poor, uneven germination; planting too
late shortens the growing season and can push flowering into a dry spell.

## Spacing and Seed Rate
For most open-pollinated and hybrid maize varieties grown by smallholders, a
common recommendation is:
- Row spacing: 75 cm between rows
- In-row spacing: 25 to 30 cm between plants (roughly 2 seeds per planting
  station, thinned to 1 strong seedling after emergence)
- This gives a target population of approximately 44,000 to 53,000 plants
  per hectare, though exact recommendations vary by variety and rainfall
  reliability — check the seed packet for hybrid-specific guidance.
Wider spacing (e.g. 90 cm rows) may be used in drier areas to reduce
competition for water; narrower spacing may be used in high-rainfall, high-
fertility conditions to raise yield potential.

## Planting Depth
Plant seed 3 to 5 cm deep in moist soil. Shallow planting in dry topsoil
risks poor germination; excessively deep planting delays and weakens
emergence, especially in heavier clay soils.

## Seed Quality
Use certified seed from a reputable supplier where possible, since certified
seed is tested for germination rate and freedom from major seed-borne
diseases. If using retained seed, conduct a simple germination test (count
germinated seeds out of 100 in moist paper towel or soil over 5 to 7 days)
before planting to estimate the seed rate needed.

## Land Preparation
Prepare land early, ideally before the first rains, to allow weed residue to
break down and to avoid delayed planting. Avoid excessive tillage on
erosion-prone slopes; minimum tillage or ridging along the contour helps
conserve soil and moisture in sloped fields.

## Intercropping
Maize is commonly intercropped with legumes such as beans or cowpea. This can
improve overall land productivity, provide some nitrogen benefit to the
following season, and offer a harvest even if one crop underperforms.
Reduce maize population slightly (about 10 to 20 percent) when intercropping
to limit competition for light and water.
